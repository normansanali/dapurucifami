Buyer's Guide: Find the Perfect Chain Earrings

Don’t be chained down by gemstone earrings; expand your jewelry collection! Learn more about finding the perfect pair of chain earrings in this short guide.

What Are Chain Earrings?

Chain earrings are earrings that feature at least one chain on them. The chain can either serve as an accessory to the earring itself or as the entire earring in some situations.

Why Should You Wear Chain Earrings?

They’re simple

Sometimes simplicity is best. You don’t need to be extravagant when going to the grocery store or picking up your laundry. And even if you want to look good, you may not like wearing something that’s a little too flashy for your tastes. Unfortunately, gem and gemstone earrings can garner a lot of attention, and it can be difficult to not stand out with them.

And that’s where chain earrings come in. While not as flashy as gemstone earrings, they still look great to wear, and people will still appreciate their beauty. With chain earrings, you’ll still somewhat stand out compared to wearing a pair of plain studs but not nearly as much as with other earring types.

They’re versatile

One of the great things about chain earrings is that they’re versatile in more ways than one. For one thing, chain earrings are, by design, very minimal, meaning that you aren’t pigeonholed into going for a certain style every time you wear them. Instead, you can dictate what you want to wear, as the earrings will, at the very least, look decent with most outfits.

Aside from its minimal design, the chains themselves can be strung and worn in various ways. Chains can easily be looped around or simply hung down from your ear in any way you wish, and you can really get creative with how you put them on. Of course, these apply to open chains, as closed chains that have both ends connected to a loop or cuff can’t really be adjusted as easily.

They’re affordable

This is arguably the main reason some people choose to go for chain earrings. Even though jewelry has become more accessible to the masses in this day and age, it can still end up being quite expensive. As such, cheaper alternatives also exist to appeal to those with tighter budgets. Chain earrings just so happen to fit the bill, as not only are they made out of affordable materials, but they’re also easy to manufacture. These factors make chain earrings very budget-friendly, and you won’t even have to spend over $50 in many cases.

Things to Consider When Buying Chain Earrings

Keep these tips in mind when thinking about buying chain earrings.

Chain types

There are three types of chain earrings and they differ based on the way the chain is connected to the frame. Firstly, you have closed chain earrings; these earrings have both ends of the chain connected to a frame, cuff, or hoop. They provide virtually zero customizability and creativity (unless the chains are detachable), but they’re also very easy to wear. You can put them on in less than a minute and be good to go.

Then, there are the open-chain earrings. These earrings have an open chain that is only connected to the frame on one end, while the other end hangs down freely. Of course, you can arrange the chain any way you wish, and this is the main advantage of open-chain earrings. The only issue is that it’ll take much longer to put on since you have to be a little more careful with setting up the chain.

Finally, there are the frameless chain earrings. Frameless chain earrings have no frame or hoop to connect to and are basically just a small chain that you wear around your ear. You’ll need at least two ear piercings to make these types of earrings work, which means not everyone can pull them off effectively.

Chain length

The length of the chain determines how long the earring will look on your ear, and in the case of open chains, also determines how much chain you’re able to work with. For closed chain earrings, you only really need to worry about how long the chain loop will look on your ear - simply pick a length that you think fits and you’re good to go.

For frameless and open-chain earrings, however, you’ll need to consider the chain length a bit more. With a short chain, you won’t be able to loop it around much, but with long chains, you’ll need more piercings to loop the chain around without it looking like it’s just hanging there. This can be difficult to nail the first time, so don’t worry if the chain feels a bit too long or too short when you first try to wear it.
