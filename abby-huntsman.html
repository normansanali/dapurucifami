Abby Huntsman- Biography

Abigail Huntsman is a journalist and television personality who was born on May 1, 1986, in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, USA. She is best known for her work as a general assignment reporter for Fox News Channel. She is currently the co-host of the show “Fox & Friends Weekend.”

What is the Net Worth of Abby Huntsman? Salary, Earnings

Huntsman has a net worth of over $2 million.

Abby Huntsman- Birth, Age, Ethnicity, Siblings

Abby was reared in Salt Lake City, Utah, among her six siblings, by her father, Jon Huntsman, Jr., a politician and the current US Ambassador to Russia, and her mother, Mary Kaye Huntsman, a well-known activist and creator of Bag of Hope and Power in You. Huntsman also spent a portion of her youth in China because her father was the US Ambassador there. She is the granddaughter of Huntsman Corporation founder Jon Huntsman, Sr. Abby has six siblings: four sisters, Asha Bharati Huntsman, who was adopted from India, Gracie Mei Huntsman, who was adopted from China, comedian Liddy Huntsman, concert pianist Mary Anne Huntsman, and two brothers, William Huntsman and Jon Huntsman, Jr., who are both serving in the United States Navy.

Education Details

Abby attended four different high schools while living in several countries such as Taiwan, Singapore, and Beijing. She played tennis in high school and twice won the state tennis championship. She studied at the University of Pennsylvania after matriculation and graduated in 2008 with a degree in Philosophy, Politics, and Economics (PPE).

Abby Huntsman- Relationship, Married Life

Abby Hutsman has been married to business analyst Jeffrey Bruce Livingston since August of 2010 when the couple married at the Washington National Cathedral. In November of 2017, Abby gave birth to their first child, a daughter named Isabel Grace Livingston. The family currently lives in New York City.

Abby Huntsman- Professional Career

Abby spent some time as a 16-year-old working behind the scenes of the show “Good Morning America,” a job she despised. She did, however, work as an intern for Diane Sawyer while in college, after which she changed her mind and decided to pursue a career as a television personality. Thus, her professional career began in 2012, during her father’s presidential campaign, when she began working as a political pundit for “Good Morning America,” “ABC News,” and “Piers Morgan Tonight,” which marked the beginning of a growth in her net worth and popularity. During the same year, she began anchoring the Huffington Post’s “HuffPost Live,” following which she was hired to host “The Cycle” from 2013 to 2015. In the meanwhile, she hosted “Politics Nation With Al Sharpton” on the MSNBC channel from 2013 to 2015.

Fox News Channel (FNC)

Abby joined Fox News Channel in 2015 as a general assignment correspondent after gaining sufficient competence and experience. The following year, she was hired as a co-host of “Fox & Friends” and “Fox & Friends Weekend,” as well as a guest co-host of another show called “America’s Newsroom.”

In 2017, Abby began serving as a guest co-host on the show “Outnumbered.” She has revealed that she will leave Fox News Channel to host “The View” on ABC.
