News: The tranquil atmosphere of Greenwood was shattered as residents grappled with the aftermath of a jolting event in their serene enclave along South Dartmouth Lane. A tragic incident, suspected to be a murder-suicide, has plunged the neighborhood into deep sorrow and disbelief.

A Couple Defined by Passion

At the heart of this tragic incident were Blase and Brooke Raia. Blase, aged 39, earned widespread respect as a diligent and well-regarded machinist at the Juniata Locomotive Shop. His coworkers praised his amiable nature and unwavering commitment to his craft. Beyond his professional life, Blase was deeply immersed in the world of dragsters, nurturing a fervent enthusiasm for speed and precision that echoed both in his work and personal pursuits.

Brooke Raia, aged 34 and previously known as Brooke Zimmerman, contributed her skills to a local chiropractic office. Much like her husband, racing was a profound passion in her life. Influenced by her father, a celebrated motorcycle racer and speedway announcer, Brooke’s affinity for the sport ran deep. The shared love for racing served as a binding force between Brooke and Blase, painting a portrait of a couple united by mutual interests and an ardor for high-octane thrills.

The Tragic Turn of Events

Their serene existence was shattered on a fateful Tuesday evening. Logan Township Police Chief Dave Hoover labeled the event as stemming from a domestic altercation. Clues found on-site suggested Blase fired a handgun at Brooke before ending his own life with a fatal shot to the head. This heart-wrenching incident unfolded within the confines of their South Dartmouth Lane residence, witnessed by four of their children. Another child returned home later, stumbling upon the harrowing aftermath about thirty minutes after the tragic events transpired. Authorities were alerted at 7:30 p.m., prompting the State police to launch a comprehensive investigation.

The Investigation and Support

The initial investigation reports confirmed that both Blase and Brooke Raia succumbed to fatal gunshot wounds to the head. Guided by Chief Hoover, the Logan Township Police Department meticulously gathered evidence to reconstruct the couple’s final moments. On the same evening, the coroner’s office was engaged in the removal of the bodies.

Law enforcement officials are painstakingly piecing together the motive behind this tragic occurrence. They are retracing the steps of Blase and Brooke on the day of the incident, conducting interviews with their children and individuals who had interactions with the couple. Their primary objective is to ascertain whether any external parties were involved in this heartbreaking incident.

In the aftermath of this sorrowful event, Blair County Children, Youth, and Family Services have taken proactive steps to safeguard the children’s well-being. They have placed the children under the care of relatives or crisis intervention specialists from UPMC Altoona, ensuring they receive the necessary assistance to navigate through the aftermath of this distressing experience.

Community Support and Generosity

The Greenwood community has united in a powerful display of unity and compassion for the survivors. Jim Patterson, a retired community member acquainted with the couple, conveyed his deep sadness and extended prayers and heartfelt thoughts to the children and the departed parents. This tragic event has underscored the tight-knit bonds and compassionate spirit prevalent among the residents.

In a bid to offer further assistance to the surviving family, two GoFundMe campaigns were promptly initiated post-incident. Within a short span, one of the campaigns had garnered over $17,000 in donations by late afternoon the following day. This remarkable outpouring of support stands as a poignant testament that, even amidst profound darkness, resilience and human kindness can shine through.
