Who Is Rachel Weisz?

Her breakthrough in cinema came with her starring roles as Evelyn Carnahan in the Hollywood action films The Mummy (1999) and The Mummy Returns (2001). Rachel Weisz appeared in several films during the 2000s, including Enemy at the Gates (2001), About a Boy (2002), Constantine (2005), The Fountain (2006), and Lovely Bones (2009).

She won the Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress for her performance as an activist in the 2005 thriller The Constant Gardener and the Laurence Olivier Award for Best Actress for her role as Blanche Dubois in the 2009 revival of A Streetcar Named Desire.

In the 2010s, Rachel Weisz continued to star in big-budget films such as the action movie The Bourne Legacy (2012) and the fantasy film Oz the Great and Powerful (2013), as well as starring in the independent film The Bourne. She received critical acclaim for her performance in The Deep Blue Sea (2011), Denied (2016), and Favorite (2018). Her portrayal of Sarah Churchill in the latter earned her a Best Supporting Actress award and her second Academy Award nomination.
