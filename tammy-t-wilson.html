Russell Wilson

Russell Wilson enrolled at ‘North Carolina State University’ in 2006 where he was the first freshman quarterback to earn all-ACC first-team honors while playing at NC State.

Coach Tom O’Brien, on the other hand, was enraged by his desire to play baseball alongside football.

Russell became part of the ‘Major League Baseball’ after the ”Colorado Rockies” in the 2010 draft. He played 32 matches for the ‘Tri-City Dust Devils’.

For his senior year of college, he transferred to the ‘University of Wisconsin,’ where he played college football during the 2011 season.

He had a fantastic senior year, leading his team to a Big Ten championship. Because of his passing abilities, he was also able to set a new NCAA record.

Due to his small frame, he was not regarded as an ideal choice to play in the NFL even back then. The popular NFL franchise ‘Seattle Seahawks’ began showing interest in him in an unexpected turn of events.

Drafted to Seattle Seahawks

Russell signed a four-year contract with the ‘Seahawks’ in May 2012, and he made his preseason debut against the ‘Kansas City Chiefs.’

With 26 touchdown passes and nine interceptions, he finished the 2013 ‘Super Bowl Season’ on a good note. He became the ”NFC Offensive Player of the Week” for the second time in his career by the 13th week.

The ‘Seahawks’ moved to the ‘Super Bowl XLVIII’ after defeating the ‘San Francisco 49ers’ in the NFC championship game.

He put up an outstanding performance and received a financial bonus of USD 169,141 from the league’s Performance-Based Program. The ‘Seahawks extended his contract for another four years in July 2015.

His play was uneven in the first few games, but he quickly improved, becoming the only quarterback in NFL history to throw three or more touchdown passes without throwing an interception in five straight matches.

Throughout the season, he broke numerous single-season marks, including the most passing yards, passer rating, and passing touchdowns. He was regarded as one of the top quarterbacks in the ‘Seahawks’.
