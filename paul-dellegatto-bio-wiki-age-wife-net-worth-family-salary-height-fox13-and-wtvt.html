Paul Dellegatto Biography and Wiki

Paul Dellegatto is an American television weather forecaster and meteorologist who was born in Natick, MA as Paul N. Dellegatto. He works as the chief meteorologist at WTVT in Tampa, Florida. Prior to WTVT, he was a Meteorologist for WGME-TV in Portland, Maine in 1984.

Dellegatto started his meteorology career by preparing weather forecasts for clients of a private weather company in Ithaca and New York.

Later he becomes the Chief Meteorologist at WXII-TV in Winston-Salem, North Carolina. Paul has been with WTVT since 1990 and took over for Roy Leep as the chief meteorologist in 1997 “he previously served as morning meteorologist until 1997. Dellegatto is AMS Certified and one of the five meteorologists that are certified broadcast meteorologists of the WTVT weather department.

On 30 August 2019, he reported on “DORIAN NOW A CAT 3 HURRICANE” on his Facebook page. “The storm is about 625 miles east of West Palm Beach Florida and is moving northwest at 20 mph. Max sustained winds are 115 mph. Dorian will likely intensify to at least a CAT 4 storm as it nears Florida on Labor Day”.

WTVT meteorologist Dellegatto overcame gloomy beginning to succeed

Just as hurricane season brings turbulent times, so did the beginning of WTVT-Ch. 13 chief meteorologist Dellegatto’s career in North Carolina.

It was his first gig as a chief meteorologist, and the rough start wasn’t for a lack of knowledge or understanding of weather; it was for something equally important: his Boston accent.

The locals couldn’t relate to a “Yankee” and the “funny” way he talked. The issue became so serious that he considered quitting meteorology altogether. The Tampa Bay Times spoke with Dellegatto about how he overcame the obstacle.
