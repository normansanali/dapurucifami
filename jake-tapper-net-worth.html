Jake Tapper | Career

In 1992, a Democratic congressional candidate hired Tapper as her campaign press secretary.

After the candidate was elected, he continued to work for her.

Jake also worked for Handgun Control Inc. in the late 1990s, as well as Powell Tate, a public relations agency in Washington, DC.

In 1998, Tapper started working as a full-time journalist after producing a series of papers as a freelancer.

He worked as a senior journalist for the Washington City Paper and was recognized for his accomplishments with a Society of Professional Journalists Award.

After that, he worked as Salon.com’s Washington correspondent from 1999 until 2002.

In 2001, he also worked as a columnist for Discussion Magazine and presented the CNN news talk show “Take Five” at this time.

Later on, Jake was hired by ABC News in 2003, where he worked for more than eight years before rejoining CNN.
