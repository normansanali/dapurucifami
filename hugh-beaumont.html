Who was Hugh Beaumont?

Eugene Hugh Beaumont was an American writer, director, and actor. He is best remembered for playing Ward Cleaver in the television sitcom “Leave It to Beaver.” He was born in Eudora, Kansas, to a traveling salesman, and his family moved frequently as a result of his father’s business. After graduating from a private coeducational prep school in Tennessee, he attended the University of Chattanooga and later earned a Master of Theology degree from the University of Southern California.

Beaumont’s career in show business began in 1931, when he made appearances on radio, in nightclubs, and in theatres. He began acting in films in 1940 and went on to appear in a number of films and television projects. In his personal life, the actor has only married once. He married actress Kathryn Adams on Easter Sunday, April 1941. They were married for 33 years before divorcing in 1974. In 1982, Beaumont suffered a heart attack while visiting his son, a psychologist stationed in Munich, West Germany.
