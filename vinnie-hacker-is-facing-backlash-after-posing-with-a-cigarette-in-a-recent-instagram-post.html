Vinnie Hacker Is Facing Backlash After Posing With A Cigarette In A Recent Instagram Post

Vinnie Hacker, an influencer and TikTok developer, is experiencing backlash after uploading a photo of himself posing with a cigarette while claiming to have stopped smoking last year. On Wednesday, January 18, 2023, Vinnie’s Instagram, @vinniehacker, published the photo in question with the caption:

“Who cares about the cigarette, will you?”

In the past, the influencer has been observed posting material while smoking. However, he intimated on Twitter in November 2022 that he would be quitting cigarettes for good. Vinnie is presently on vacation in Paris with a buddy and fellow influencer Noah Beck, and they have been exchanging images.

Fans were unhappy with the TikToker after viewing photographs of Vinnie Hacker smoking. Internet people quickly reacted to the message, expressing their dismay at his previous acts. Some internet users questioned if Hacker had forgotten about his prior commitment to stop smoking, while others simply told him that smoking was bad for him.

Who exactly is Vinnie Hacker?

Vincent Cole ‘Vinnie’ Hacker, an American social media star, was born in July 2002. He became famous after posting lip-syncing videos on his TikTok account, @vhackerr. Vinnie attended O’Dea High School in Seattle, Washington, where he played baseball. He joined the video-sharing site in 2019 and has had over 21 million followers since then. Purgatory, the influencer’s apparel line, will be released in December 2020. Hoodies, t-shirts, sweatpants, and caps are among the limited-edition goods available from the company. Vinnie is also involved in Hype House, a reality program that chronicles the lives of numerous social media influencers and personalities.

On his Twitter account, @vinniehacker, Vinnie Hacker stated that he was becoming “vape free” in November 2022. Although vapes are non-tobacco e-cigarettes, they do contain nicotine and are classified as tobacco products by the Food and Drug Administration (FDA). The influencer has given frequent updates on being “vape-free” and exhibiting hopeful signs of quitting in November. Fans who supported the former baseball star in his fight applauded the action. Vinnie has yet to respond to the situation.
